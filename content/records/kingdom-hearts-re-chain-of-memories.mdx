:::note Believe in the heart of the cards.
This is one of THE best entries in the series, not only cause they somehow experimented with a completely different system and made it work, but also because of the plot.
Yes, there is a GBA version and no, I'll end it at that.

Game was pretty straight forward, its Kingdom Heart 1.5 so we're not in Kingdom Hearts 2 but the even prior to it or at least a parallel story to what was happening in other titles.
Gameplay was different, they replaced the action combat from the first one and made it more strategic but still action.
You have a deck of cards, that you can edit with different cards that you find around the world, unlock or buy.
Cards can heal you, refresh the deck, debuff, buff.. sounds familiar? Yeah I think KH Union X definitely was doing something.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-re-chain-of-memories/1778854180957-level-up-menu-from-rcom.webp)

Your deck had to be clever, you can't just have random cards or you'll waste time and do no damage... so you had to mix and match, have multiple copies of some cards.. it's a lot.
Before attacking, you could stack up to 3 cards, to make a big value and have different effect/stronger attack.
Stacking different cards, sometimes gave you special attack.. so some deck revolved around stacking as many copies of specific cards as possible.
During bosses, you'd have to cancel their attack by either matching the value of their attack:
If they played 2 cards for the value of 18, you could stop them by playing something equal.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-re-chain-of-memories/1778854139954-screenshot-2025-02-06-6-20-36-pm-1200x676.jpg)

Soo.....the fun was managing your deck, while also controlling the character, cause you can't just stand still.. duh.
And it was definitely something.

Plot wise, we see lots of faces that we know already if you played the DS game and some new ones.
Sora is wandering through this castle and the deeper he gets, the more memories he loses.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-re-chain-of-memories/1778854163662-kingdom-hearts-re-chain-of-memories-sony-playstation-2-ps2-video-game-5.webp)

It was definitely a different story from what we were used to and it was getting more intricate but more interesting too ~
The very cool thing they did is.. game was not done once you beat it with sora, you could now play through Riku's POV!
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-re-chain-of-memories/1778854260705-450px-mm-miracle-lv3-khrecom.gif)

Now, it would be hypocritical of me if I didn't mention BBB, since I complained about it having pretty much same story with different POVs and repeating same game world loop.
This one wasn't gamebreaking but I think it was handled differently, yes Riku had different cards and powers compared to Sora but boss fights were also different, so you were playing a completely different game almost.
BBB main issue is that it was pretty much a 1:1 but with different characters, which for ME.. wasn't enough to justify replaying the game 2 whole times.

I think up until this point, it felt like (at least back then) that what they envisioned/path was different, because this games and KH2 are still giving me that KH vibes that is somewhat... I wouldn't say lost replaced/overshadowed by something else.
Plus it felt like a very well thought game.
