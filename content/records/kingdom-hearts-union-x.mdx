:::note I've been having this weird thoughts lately
I played this game from the moment in came out and all the way till it died... I even spent money, not much of course but still.
I'm not gonna talk about Dark Road, as I simply gave up at that point and just checked the updates from other people.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778851730951-wp-1472869322835.png)

This game was simple, move your character around by dragging your finger across the screen, engage with enemies on the map and it would then be a turn based fight.
To attack, you had medals that once you swiped up would activate and do something, a skill which did damage or buffed you, debuffed the enemy or as game went by, all 3 of those.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778850306027-maxresdefault.jpg)

Then, what's special about it?
Customization, you could make your own character and make it look goofy as hell or mix and match different outfits, if you were lame you could've just looked like Sora, Riku or whoever.
In this game, you pulled for 'medals' the equivalent of an SSR Card or an SSR Character, effects where not particularly crazy, pretty simple but cool looking stuff..
It was the numbers and the effect that came with them.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778850314682-kingdomheartsunion-2475703b.png)

So, early on medals were pretty straight forward, they'd either:
Damage, Heal, Buff you, Debuff enemy or a combination of those, until they became crazy later on.
This game had elements, each medal carried an element so you had to play around advantages, not just that.. you had keyblades and they were all different and, late game it was pretty much a must to use the correct combination of keyblade/medals.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778850278749-kh-union-x-header.jpg)

Now, it's a gacha.. don't get excited.
That meant, if you pulled the newest medal, you'd be set for this rotation of weekly/monthly content, get all the cool resources, free pulls and cosmetic with ease.
If you didn't.. ggs you're screwed.

And did I mention PVP? lets just.. lets just move on.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778851190523-pvp-sample3.png)

BUT HOLD ON, you thought pulling the medal was it? XDDDD LMAOOOO
Nuh uh, you needed copies and the only alternative to that was an item, that could've been used instead of a copy BUT, it was extremely rare and scarce.
So even if you got the medal, you'd have to pull for copies or it would simply not perform as it should.

Now, game was quite dry in terms of content and very very greedy, I remember seeing videos of people dropping 5-10k to pull for medals, clearing the newest event and then.. just nothing.
Overtime it got better.. more rewards, more freebies but at the same time more greed Square Enix.
It was absolutely way better half way through its life, events were somewhat doable, lots of free resources distributed, guilds meant something.
Like, there was a lot and the game was actually enjoyable.
Obviously though, if you were not strong enough you could NOT progress, which meant important story parts were locked behind luck and money.
![](/media/records/kingdom-hearts-union-x/1778851622616-kingdom-hearts-backup-data.jpg)

At that time, we were all collectively waiting for KH3.. so outside of the gacha, replaying old titles with mods and theory crafting, we had nothing.
Despite that, I really liked this game and I wish Square Enix made a replacement for it.
The game ran for quite a while, however they basically ran the game as a placeholder for KH3, it was something to entertain and make money while people were waiting for the main title.
Like I said, it was a gacha and a greedy one at that but nothing as bad as some gatchas we have today.

Because there were factions and a leader board, pvp, raids etc.. the game was basically a weekly chore where you had to keep up with new releases or get thrashed by the last boss with 191239 bars of HP and the people in PVP.
That meant less rewards, less pulls, more FOMO.

The game was genuinely fun and it made people feel part of KH world, despite the greed I think it was a very fun experience and I'm glad that I can say I was there.
